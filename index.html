My Development Journey


My journey to become a web developer has been an interesting one. In 2011 my bags were packed and I head out to New England where I started to attend the New England Art Institute to pursue a bachelors in photography. As time went on and as I progress through my course work, I was drawn to photomanipulation and noticed what I was able to do.

From there I switched my major to graphic design. As a design student I eventually found my way into web design. After a few semesters as a design student I found myself packing up my bags to head to the opposite coast; I was headed to Oregon to attend the sister school for web design. After a few months life punched me in the face hard! I wasn't able to complete my education and wound up in my home town. For a while I was hooking and jabbing with what life had to throw at me and found a position as a web & graphic coordinator for Hospice of Yuma. After a year or so, I found a different job as a graphic designer / social media marketer for Bio Clinic. During both of these time frames I was a free-lance graphic designer. It took a few friends along the way to help me realize once again what I wanted to do.

With their help my dream found a spark once again. I signed up for a coding bootcamp through WOZ-U where I was able to complete my education. I met a few friends along the way and now I am a co-founder of Red Eye Tech developing tools to make life a little easier.
