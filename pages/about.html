More Details:


The journey was interesting to say the least. Becoming a veteran (x2), to graphic designer and content creator to developer. So, why so many changes? I am a firm believer that you have to go after your dreams no matter how small or big they may be. And to go after them the moment they are formed. When I was 10 I already knew what I wanted to do when I grew up; which was to become a Marine. And it was so. As my contract came to an end, I wanted to be a graphic designer. I may have started out in photography but I shifted my way back to graphic design and it was so. In my design career, my interest began to shift once again. I found my self becoming more and more interested in coding and technology; and wanted to become a developer. However, it didn't happen overnight.

Of course life took it's course and many things happened along the way whether they were favorable or unfavorable, but my end goal never changed. I overcame the physical challenges that laid before me and didn't let anything get in the way of my goal(s). With everything that I have experienced, I'm starting to the think development doesn't start at the computer but within our lives. Anyone can hop on the computer and start coding and then call themselves a developer. But why not develope projects that makes an impact on someone's life or find away to make life a little easier with usuful tools, and connect people to those tools. This is what I intend to do as a developer. And let it be so.
